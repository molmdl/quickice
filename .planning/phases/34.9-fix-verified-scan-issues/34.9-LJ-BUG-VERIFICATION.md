## Comb-rule 1 vs GAFF2 Compatibility Analysis

### Background: What the Combining Rule Affects

Under GROMACS nbfunc=1 (Lennard-Jones), both comb-rule 1 and comb-rule 2 use the **same column format** in `[atomtypes]`: V = σ (nm), W = ε (kJ/mol). The difference is **only in how cross-interaction terms are computed** when `gen-pairs=yes`:

| comb-rule | σ cross-term | ε cross-term | Convention |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| 1 | σ_ij = √(σ_i × σ_j) | ε_ij = √(ε_i × ε_j) | Geometric (GROMOS) |
| 2 | σ_ij = (σ_i + σ_j) / 2 | ε_ij = √(ε_i × ε_j) | Lorentz-Berthelot (AMBER/OPLS) |

**Key point:** The ε combining rule is **IDENTICAL** (geometric) for both. Only the σ combining differs.

### GROMACS Force Field Conventions (from installed GROMACS 2023.5)

| Force Field | comb-rule | gen-pairs | fudgeLJ | fudgeQQ |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|---------|---------|
| **AMBER99SB-ILDN** | **2** | yes | 0.5 | 0.8333 |
| OPLS-AA/L | 3 | yes | 0.5 | 0.5 |
| GROMOS54a7 | 1 | no | 1.0 | 1.0 |
| **QuickIce** | **1** | **yes** | **0.5** | **0.8333** |

**QuickIce uses a hybrid approach**: AMBER-like fudge factors (fudgeLJ=0.5, fudgeQQ=0.8333) with GROMOS-like combining rule (comb-rule=1). This departs from the standard AMBER convention (comb-rule=2) used by all GROMACS-bundled AMBER force fields.

### Was the Old Code "Doubly Wrong"?

**No — the "doubly wrong" characterization was inaccurate.** Under both comb-rule 1 and comb-rule 2 (with nbfunc=1), the V/W columns in `[atomtypes]` are σ and ε. The old code with comb-rule=2 wrote σ/ε values in the correct column format — the problem was purely the **wrong magnitudes** (1000× too small for σ, 10⁶× for ε), not a column interpretation error.

The comb-rule change from 2→1 is a **secondary design choice** that affects cross-term computation, not column format correctness.

### Physical Significance Assessment

**Context for the Δσ magnitudes:**

| Scale | Physical meaning |
|-------|-----------------|
| <0.1% | Within numerical precision of force field parameters |
| 0.1–1% | Within typical force field fitting accuracy |
| 1–2% | Comparable to Madrid2019 ion model parameter uncertainty |
| 2–5% | Approach force field transferability limits |
| >5% | Requires scientific justification |
| ~30% | Appears large but can be physically irrelevant for weak interactions |

**Key physical observations:**

1. **Self-interactions are IDENTICAL**: For OW_ice × OW_ice, geometric mean = arithmetic mean = σ_OW = 0.31668 nm. The combining rule does not affect like-atom interactions.

2. **Most important water-guest pairs**: The dominant thermodynamic interactions between ice water and guest molecules are:
   - OW_ice × c3 (CH4 carbon): **Δσ = 0.062%** — completely negligible
   - OW_ice × os (THF oxygen): **Δσ = 0.000%** — identical (σ values nearly equal)
   - OW_ice × o_2 (CO2 oxygen): **Δσ = 0.025%** — completely negligible
   - These pairs determine **hydrate cage stability** and are unaffected.

3. **Hydrogen cross-terms**: The H-atom cross-terms are less important because:
   - H atoms have small ε values (~0.087 kJ/mol)
   - H atoms are typically shielded by larger C/O atoms
   - Even the 0.5–0.9% difference for hc/h1 is within force field accuracy

4. **Ion cross-terms**: The NA/CL cross-terms show 1.5–1.9% differences:
   - This is comparable to the Madrid2019 model's own uncertainty (~1-3%)
   - The Madrid2019 model was parameterized for TIP4P/2005, not TIP4P-ICE
   - The force field mismatch already introduces larger uncertainty than the combining rule

5. **The ho outlier**: The 29.5% Δσ for ho (ethanol hydroxyl H) is a special case:
   - ε_cross = 0.13 kJ/mol — the total LJ interaction is negligible regardless of combining rule
   - Hydrogen bonding is dominated by electrostatics (~20 kJ/mol), not LJ (~0.1 kJ/mol)
   - The ho atomtype's σ = 0.054 nm is intentionally tiny (represents a vanishing vdW radius for a hydrogen-bond donor)
   - **Conclusion: The large Δσ% does NOT translate to a physically significant difference**

- There is no "ground truth" for these cross-terms — both rules are approximations
- The ~1-2% difference from rule choice is within force field transferability limits

## GAP: comb-rule 1 is non-standard for AMBER/GAFF2

**Status:** open
**Severity:** P2 (scientific convention violation, not a simulation-breaking bug)
**Affects:** All 6 TOP-writing functions in gromacs_writer.py

### Problem

The LJ bugfix (34.9-02) changed `comb-rule` from 2 (Lorentz-Berthelot) to 1 (geometric) in all 6 TOP-writing functions. This change was based on an incorrect claim that "comb-rule 2 requires C6/C12 format in [atomtypes]" — in fact, BOTH comb-rule 1 and 2 use sigma/epsilon format. Only comb-rule 3 uses C6/C12.

The bug was in the VALUES (0.31668e-3 instead of 3.16680e-1), not the combining rule. The correct fix should have been: keep comb-rule 2, correct the sigma/epsilon values.

### Why it matters

- AMBER/GAFF2 force fields in GROMACS use comb-rule 2 (Lorentz-Berthelot) — this is the convention TIP4P-ICE and GAFF2 were parameterized with
- The `.bak` ITP header explicitly says "add to [atomtypes] of ffnonbonded.itp under AMBER forcefield" — AMBER convention
- comb-rule 1 produces sigma cross-terms that are 0-2% different from comb-rule 2
- Publications comparing QuickIce output against AMBER literature will show systematic deviations
- GROMACS doesn't reject comb-rule 1, so grompp tests pass — but it's conventionally wrong
